## Research Summary

### The Execution Gap Defining 2026

The headline number from McKinsey's State of AI 2025 sums up the current moment: **88% of enterprises use AI in at least one function**, up from 78% in 2024 and just 55% in 2023. But only about one-third have actually scaled AI enterprise-wide. That gap — between adoption and execution — is the defining story of 2026.

Redwood Software frames it as a maturity shift. The debate over whether AI belongs in automation strategy is "effectively over." The real questions now are: Can automation adapt when reality deviates from plan? Can leaders rely on it under pressure? Does it make the business stronger, not just faster?

The problem isn't technology readiness. It's integration and governance at scale. Agentic initiatives struggle when they operate in isolation — confined to a single team or experimental environment. The value only materializes when AI, ERP systems, and orchestration platforms work as one connected system.

### Verticals Leading the Charge

Manufacturing, logistics, and defense are pulling ahead. Deloitte's 2026 enterprise AI report notes adoption is "especially advanced" in these sectors, where robotics, autonomous vehicles, and drones are already reshaping operations. These aren't pilot programs — they're production deployments generating measurable ROI.

The GCC region shows similar patterns: 84% adoption but only 11% "value realizers" (companies linking AI to at least 5% of earnings). The takeaway: adoption means nothing without the operational infrastructure to extract value.

### AI Governance: From Policy to Operating Model

Shadow AI is the emerging risk. As AI moves from experimentation to deployment, governance that lives only in policy documents will slow adoption. Governance built directly into workflows accelerates trust and scale. Redwood predicts that by 2026, effective governance will look more like an embedded operating model — with defined boundaries for autonomous action, escalation paths for human oversight, and transparent model validation that scales across complex cross-functional processes.

### The Architecture Shift

AIERA breaks down why agentic systems are fundamentally different from chatbots. They operate via a planning loop: receive a goal, decompose into sub-tasks, select appropriate tools, execute sequentially or in parallel, evaluate output, and iterate. This is a different architecture entirely. Gartner predicted 25% of enterprises would deploy agentic AI by 2028 — current adoption velocity suggests that timeline is compressing significantly.
