# AI Automation Research — 2026-04-27

## AI Automation Trends: The Execution Era

2026 is the year AI automation stops being experimental and starts being operational. Here's what the data and industry moves actually show.

---

### The Numbers

- **88%** of organizations now use AI automation in at least one function (McKinsey, up from 78% in 2024)
- **71%** use generative AI, but only ~33% have scaled it enterprise-wide
- AI infrastructure spending hit **$47.4B** in H1 2024, up 97% year-over-year
- The AI automation market is projected to reach **$223B by 2028**

There's a clear execution gap. Most companies are still in pilot-to-production, not full-scale deployment.

---

### Key Trends Driving the Market

**Agentic AI is mainstream.** Gartner moved it from "emerging" to "mainstream adoption" category. The shift: from asking AI questions to delegating multi-step tasks. Agents now decompose goals, call tools, execute in parallel, and self-correct. OpenAI's Agents SDK, Google Gemini agent frameworks, and open-source stacks like CrewAI have made deployment accessible without dedicated ML teams.

**The market is consolidating around major players.** Microsoft, Google Cloud, Salesforce, IBM, and AWS are all pushing enterprise AI agents hard in 2026. Adobe launched a customer experience AI agent platform in April. Accenture-Databricks and Adobe-AWS partnerships are accelerating enterprise adoption infrastructure.

**ROI is real but uneven.** 71% of marketing and sales teams report revenue gains. But only 39% of enterprises report measurable EBIT impact — most gains stay under 5%.

---

### What This Means

The competitive window isn't about who has AI anymore — everyone's experimenting. The gap is **execution velocity**. Companies that can move from pilot to production reliably will capture the value. Everyone else is stuck in the gap.

For a lean agency or consultancy like AnovaGrowth, this is actually the ideal positioning: the big players are selling enterprise infrastructure, but mid-market businesses need help actually deploying it.
