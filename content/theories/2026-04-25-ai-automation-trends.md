## What's Actually Happening

**1. Agentic AI is the headline.** AI agents that plan and execute multi-step workflows autonomously have moved from experimental to default. Amazon's recent 16K corporate layoffs reflect this shift — middle management and admin roles being replaced by "agentic" workflows. The agentic AI market alone is projected at $196.6B by 2034 (43.8% CAGR).

**2. Single agents → multi-agent systems.** This is the big architectural shift. Companies are moving from isolated AI tools to coordinated networks of specialized agents that communicate continuously, share context, and align decisions across functions (CRM, ERP, customer support). Think concurrent orchestration, not sequential automation. Enterprises doing this securely and transparently will gain real competitive advantage.

**3. Autonomous workflows replace rule-based bots.** Old automation needed static logic and constant human upkeep. Autonomous workflows combine AI, ML, and RPA to execute complex processes dynamically — adapting as conditions change without manual intervention.

**4. Domain-specific small language models (SLMs under 7B params) are gaining traction** — cheaper, faster, effective for targeted business tasks.

**5. SMBs are catching up.** Platforms like n8n, Zapier, and Claude Enterprise now make AI agents accessible below $50/mo. For companies with 500+ monthly customer touchpoints, these tools are becoming the default stack.

---

## The Augmentation vs. Automation Tension

HBR (April 2026) flags a key strategic split: companies chasing bottom-line gains through headcount reduction vs. those using AI to grow top-line revenue through augmentation. The data suggests augmentation-first strategies win long-term — but most businesses are splitting resources between both.
